## what i've worked on

i've had the chance to work at some interesting places. here's what actually stuck with me from each one.

<br/>

**Evermount Partners** — _digital infrastructure PE_

this is where i got obsessed with data centers. built out a benchmarking model across 15+ public comps, tracked hyperscaler capex trends, and ended up writing a full market intelligence piece on AI-driven demand. i learned how to translate macro narratives into actual investment theses.

<br/>

**PNTHR Funds** — _long/short equity hedge fund_

wrote over 30 investment memos here. pitched 5 ideas directly to partners. also got frustrated enough with the manual data workflow that i automated most of it in Python — cut prep time from ~3 hours per memo to under 30 minutes. necessity really is the mother of invention.

<br/>

**Fund Launch Partners** — _multi-sector PE_

built LBO models for multifamily real estate and media deals. learned how GP/LP structures actually work in practice, not just in textbooks. spent a lot of time in Excel and came out the other side with genuine appreciation for a clean debt waterfall.

<br/>

**Project Destined** — _real estate PE_

early exposure to CRE underwriting and deal analysis. this is where the modeling fundamentals clicked.

## things i've built

> i try to build tools that solve problems i actually have.

<br/>

**📊 financial modeling automation**
`python · bloomberg api · excel`

i was tired of spending 3 hours pulling data before i could even start thinking about an idea. so i wrote scripts to do it for me. now i spend that time on the actual analysis. (repo coming soon)

**🏢 data center market intelligence dashboard**
`python · power bi · sql`

built at evermount to benchmark AI infrastructure demand across the public comp set. the goal was to make the data easy enough to show in a partner meeting without 40 slides of context. (repo coming soon)

**🏗️ LBO model suite**
`excel · vba`

full LBO templates for lower middle-market deals — multifamily and media. debt waterfall, IRR/MOIC sensitivity, GP/LP splits. used live in real due diligence. not glamorous. useful.

**📝 investment memo generator**
`python · nlp · edgar api`

automated the annoying parts of memo writing — scraping filings, summarizing 10-Ks, building structure. used it to pitch 5 ideas at PNTHR. the analysis was still all me; the formatting wasn't.
